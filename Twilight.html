Twilight
Fiction 22Mins Tamil Director Art Director Sound Designer Graphic Designer Script Writer
Synopsis

Twilight is a personal film. It has got borrowed imagery from Sylvia Plath’s Poems. It’s an exploration of the innerscape of a person, through imagescape and the soundscape. Pop culture has hailed Sylvia as a feminist. But for me she is a Self whose end is to create. I relate only to that. Such a Self is beyond any demarcation. Her Self is an artistic one, which tends to destruct itself. We can find similar artistic Selves in the spiritual traditions all over the world.There is a subtle difference between artistic Self and a spiritually artistic Self. In an artistic Self, ego is not dissolved. We can know this from the lines of Plath where she says I am silver and exact. I have no preconceptions. Whatever I see I swallow immediately Just as it is, unmisted by love or dislike. I am not cruel, only truthful The eye of a little god, four-cornered.

But in a spiritually artistic Self, the ego is completely dissolved. We can know this from the lines of Mahadeviakka where she says You are like milk in water: I cannot tell what comes before, what after; which is the master, which the slave; what’s big, what’s small. I’ve played as you’ve played I’ve spoken as you’ve told me I’ve been as you’ve let me be O engineer of the world lord white as jasmine I’ve run Till u cried halt.

I am not here to judge, which is correct as long as the aim is to know your self. I would like to quote Rabia al-Basri (Rabia al- Adawiyya) I carry a torch in one hand And a bucket of water in the other: With these things I am going to set fire to Heaven And put out the flames of Hell So that voyagers to God can rip the veils And see the real goal.
